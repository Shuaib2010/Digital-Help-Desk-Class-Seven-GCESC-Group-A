Code: SM1- Spamming

Solution and precaution to these:

• Use spam filters. Many email providers and other messaging platforms have built-in spam filters that can help to block unwanted messages. However, even the best spam filters can miss some spam, so it is important to be vigilant.
• Be cautious about giving out your email address. Only give out your email address to trusted websites and organizations. If you receive spam messages from an unknown sender, do not reply to them and do not click on any links in the message.
• Avoid clicking on suspicious links. Phishing emails and other spam messages often contain links that, when clicked, can take you to malicious websites that can steal your personal information or infect your computer with malware. If you receive a message with a link, hover over the link to see the actual URL before clicking on it. If the URL does not look legitimate, do not click on it.
• Opt out of mailing lists. If you start receiving spam messages from a company or organization, you can usually opt out of their mailing list by clicking on an unsubscribe link at the bottom of the message.
• Install cybersecurity software. Cybersecurity software can help to protect your computer from malware and other threats, including spam. Make sure to keep your cybersecurity software up to date with the latest security patches
