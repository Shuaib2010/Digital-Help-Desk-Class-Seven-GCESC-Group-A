Code: P1- Plagiarism

Solution and precaution to these:

• Be careful about what you copy and paste. When you are copying and pasting text from a website, be sure to check the source of the information. If you are not sure whether the information is reliable, do not use it.
• Be careful about using images and videos. If you are using images or videos in your work, be sure to obtain permission from the copyright holder.
• Be careful about paraphrasing other people's work. If you are paraphrasing someone else's work, be sure to cite them properly and to make sure that your paraphrase is accurate and does not misrepresent the original work.
• Be aware of the different types of plagiarism. There are many different ways to plagiarize, so it is important to be aware of them all. Some common types of plagiarism include:
o Direct plagiarism: This is when you copy someone else's work word for word.
o Paraphrasing plagiarism: This is when you take someone else's ideas and rephrase them in your own words without attribution.
o Mosaic plagiarism: This is when you piece together different pieces of someone else's work to create a new work without attribution.
• Use proper citation. When you use someone else's work in your own work, be sure to cite them properly. This means giving them credit for their work and providing a link or reference to the original source.
• Use plagiarism detection software. There are a number of plagiarism detection software programs available that can help you identify instances of plagiarism in your work. These programs can be especially helpful for students and researchers
