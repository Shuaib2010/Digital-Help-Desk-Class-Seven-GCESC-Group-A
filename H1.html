Code: H1- Hacking

Solution and precaution to these:

• Use a strong password and don't reuse it across multiple accounts. A strong password is at least 12 characters long and includes a mix of upper and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. You can use a password manager to help you create and manage strong passwords for all of your accounts.
• Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible.2FA adds an extra layer of security to your account by requiring you to enter a code from your phone in addition to your password when logging in.
• Be careful about what links you click on and what attachments you open. Phishing emails are a common way for hackers to gain access to accounts. Phishing emails often contain links that appear to go to legitimate websites, but in reality they take you to fake websites that look like the real thing. When in doubt, don't click on the link or open the attachment.
• Keep your software up to date. Software updates often include security patches that can help protect your devices from known vulnerabilities.
• Be careful about where you use public Wi-Fi networks. Public Wi-Fi networks are not always secure, so it's best to avoid using them for sensitive tasks like logging into your bank account or checking your email. If you do need to use a public Wi-Fi network, be sure to use a VPN to encrypt your traffic
